Bangladesh is a South Asian country located on the Bay of Bengal, bordered by India and Myanmar. Despite its relatively small size, it is one of the most densely populated nations in the world, with a population of over 170 million. The country gained independence in 1971 after a nine-month liberation war against Pakistan. Dhaka, its capital, is the political, cultural, and economic hub. Bengali is the official language, and Islam is the major religion, followed by Hinduism and other faiths. Bangladesh has made remarkable progress in recent decades, particularly in the garment and textile industry, which is the backbone of its economy. It is also known for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant traditions, and natural beauty, including the Sundarbans mangrove forest and Cox’s Bazar, the world’s longest natural sea beach. Despite challenges such as poverty, climate change, and natural disasters, Bangladesh continues to grow rapidly, earning global recognition as one of the fastest-developing economies.
